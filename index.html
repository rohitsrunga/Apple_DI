  • As expected, sales increase during holiday weeks. A closer look reveals that Thanksgiving and the week before Christmas yield the highest consumer activity
  • Promotional markdowns tend to lead to steeper returns during holiday weeks, except for Promo2 and Promo3
  • Promo5 should be reconsidered as its relationship with sales seems indeterminate
  • Store type and size each have strong correlations with weekly sales
  • There does not seem to be strong relationships between sales and core macroeconomic factors
  • Fuel prices and Temperatures may have an association with sales at the extreme ends of their distributions

Recommendations & Insights

Modeling Methodology

Random Forest offers a good balance between interpretability and performance

Promotional Markdowns

Most Promotional Markdowns display varying levels of success given the time of year; Promo5 may need to be reevaluated

Store Type and Department Variability

Resource allocations will need to account for department and store types, as they play significant roles in predicting weekly sales

Impact of Supply on Demand

In both the EDA and model development phases, there did not seem to be a strong relationship between inventory levels and sales

Exogenous Factors

Macroeconomic and environmental variables did not provide a signifiance lift in explaining weekly sales

Holiday Definitions

We recommend working with the business to more thoroughly define holidays and significant temporal factors
